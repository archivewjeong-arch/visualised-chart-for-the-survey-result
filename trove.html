About Trove

National Library of Australia

Trove is a free online research portal providing digitised newspapers, government records, books, maps, and parliamentary documents from cultural and research collections across Australia. Students explore Australian democracy, law reform, and national identity through historical newspapers and government gazettes.

Academic Justification

Mishra and Koehler (2006) — TPACK

Locating information from Australian civic sources is simultaneously a disciplinary and digital literacy practice, achieving a meaningful intersection of technological, pedagogical, and content knowledge.

Falloon (2020) — TDC Framework

Information literacy as the first personal-professional competency within the TDC framework encompasses recognising information needs, using effective search strategies, and evaluating and organising findings — all of which Trove's search sequence directly develops.

Puentedura (2013) — SAMR
Modification

As students access digitised primary sources such as original parliamentary records and historical newspapers, they can transform the scope and authenticity of civic inquiry beyond textbooks.
