About Thinglink

Free interactive image platform

Students take or source an image of an Australian federal ballot paper (House of Representatives and Senate) and configure clickable hotspots over it, layering text, audio, and video to explain each stage of the voting process: how to number preferences, how preferential voting works, what makes a vote formal or informal, and how votes are counted to form government.

Academic Justification

Falloon (2020) — TDC Framework

Both technical competence (mechanics of operating digital technologies) and technological competence (understanding the role and potential of digital tools) in the TDC framework develop simultaneously as students operate Thinglink's tagging features while judging which voting concepts each hotspot should carry.

Mishra and Koehler (2006) — TPACK

Students can only place meaningful hotspots on the ballot paper if they understand preferential voting itself. Technological and content knowledge are developed together.

Puentedura (2013) — SAMR
Modification

A static ballot paper is redesigned into a layered, interactive explainer that embeds multimedia and reveals the voting process on demand — significantly redesigning the original task.
