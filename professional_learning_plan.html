1
Civics to life through hands-on digital tools
Description
Bringing Year 8 Civics to life through hands-on digital tools that mirror real democratic systems. Students will use PEO and the AEC's interactive voting tool to experience preferential voting step by step.
Linked to: AC9HC8K02, AC9HC8S02, Investigating element (ACARA, 2022b)
PEO AEC voting tool iCivics
Weeks
Week 3Explore PEO, AEC voting tool, and iCivics; identify relevant Year 8 resources.
Week 4Design and trial a mock preferential voting activity using a shared digital platform.
Week 6Refine the activity and map it to the Year 8 curriculum content description.
Success criteria
A fully mapped activity ready to use, with clear links to AC9HC8K02 and a short reflection on what worked and what I would change.
2
Digital newsletter on civic life in Australia
Description
Teaching Year 8 students to create and publish a digital newsletter about how individuals and groups contribute to civic life. Real readers shift the task from assignment to message — publishing for an audience gives students a reason to care.
Linked to: AC9HC8K05, AC9HC8S05, Creating and Exchanging element (ACARA, 2022b)
Canva Adobe Express
Weeks
Week 3Research Canva and Adobe Express; identify a relevant civic participation topic aligned to AC9HC8K05.
Week 4Create a sample digital newsletter myself to understand each tool's strengths and limitations.
Week 5Develop a teaching sequence guiding students from researching a civic issue to publishing a finished newsletter.
Success criteria
My own sample newsletter produced and a teaching sequence ready to use in a Year 8 Civics and Citizenship classroom, with clear links to AC9HC8K05.
3
Digital project management for collaborative tasks
Description
Building real structure into collaborative tasks in Year 8 Civics through digital project management tools. Managing group work is something I find genuinely challenging to teach well, and I want to change that.
Linked to: AC9HC8S04, AC9HC8S05, Managing and Operating element (ACARA, 2022b)
Microsoft Teams Google Docs Trello
Weeks
Week 3Explore Microsoft Teams, Google Docs, or Trello and evaluate each for student collaboration in a Civics classroom.
Week 4Try using one tool myself and note what students need to explicitly learn.
Week 6Design a basic setup for role management, progress tracking, and digital submission of group work.
Success criteria
A framework that works in a real classroom, with explicit links to AC9HC8S04 and AC9HC8S05, and a short list of digital literacy skills to explicitly teach students before the task begins.
